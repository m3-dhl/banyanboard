# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small software teams (2–10 people) who need visual task tracking without the overhead of enterprise tools. They open the board, scan column state, move cards, and close the tab. The primary interaction is spatial and gestural — not form-based. Context: focused work sessions, often alongside an editor or terminal.

## Product Purpose

BanyanBoard is a self-hosted kanban board that gets out of the way. It exists because small teams don't need Jira's power and don't want its weight. Success looks like: zero setup friction, cards moved without a second thought, no features that require reading docs.

## Brand Personality

Calm. Focused. Unassuming. The product never competes with the work itself. It has opinions — it knows what it is and what it isn't — but it expresses them through what it leaves out, not through visual drama.

## Anti-references

- **Jira** — Heavy, enterprise, bureaucratic. Every workflow a ritual. Every screen a configuration maze. BanyanBoard is the antidote.
- **Over-designed tools that perform productivity** — Products where the interface is the feature, where animations announce themselves, where chrome crowds the work. The board should disappear.

## Design Principles

1. **The interface disappears behind the work.** Every element on screen earns its place by making the next drag or the next card easier. Nothing decorative.
2. **Trust the user.** Small teams don't need guardrails, warnings, or confirmation dialogs. Assume intent, make actions fast, enable undo over prevention.
3. **Clarity over cleverness.** If a UI pattern needs explaining, it's wrong. Standard affordances, consistent vocabulary, no invented interactions.
4. **Quiet quality.** Details that reward attention — spacing, micro-transitions, honest feedback — not decoration. Right, not remarkable.
5. **Self-hosted confidence.** The product knows it's not Jira. It's not apologizing for being small. It's proud of being focused.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigation throughout (cards, columns, forms). ARIA roles and live regions for activity feed. Reduced-motion alternative for all transitions. Color is never the sole information carrier.
